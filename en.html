I am Tin Yeh Huang, currently pursuing a Bachelor of Engineering (Honours) in Product Engineering with Secondary Major in Innovation and Entrepreneurship at the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, while serving as an 01 Scholar at Qian Xuesen Mechanics Class - Shenzhen X-Institute, Tsinghua University. My research interests span across multiple cutting-edge fields including artificial intelligence, mathematics, neuroscience, and computational biology, dedicated to exploring innovative possibilities through interdisciplinary integration.

In academic research, I focus on applying artificial intelligence technologies to biomedical and social innovation fields, participating in multiple national-level research projects, including global heatwave disaster adaptation factor extraction and analysis based on multimodal BERT models, and research on the application of AI digital twin technology in addressing elderly companionship issues.

I firmly believe that technology should serve human welfare. Through interdisciplinary research approaches, I explore the potential of artificial intelligence in solving real-world social problems, hoping to contribute to advancing technological innovation and social progress.

🔬 Research Projects (Public)

Global Heatwave Disaster Adaptation Factor Extraction and Analysis Based on Multimodal BERT Model

Project Type: 2024 CAS Undergraduate Innovation and Practice Training Program (National-level Project) | Duration: 2024.11 - Ongoing

Research Content: This research integrates text, image, and structured data based on multimodal BERT models to accurately identify key factors affecting heatwave adaptation. The research results will provide solid theoretical and data support for formulating scientific global heatwave response strategies and enhance society's overall disaster adaptation capacity.

Supervisor: Researcher Ge Yong (Institute of Geographic Sciences and Natural Resources Research, CAS, Distinguished Young Scholar)

Large Language Models Spatial Analysis BERT Disaster Management

Research on Elderly Companionship Issues Based on AI Digital Twin Technology

Project Type: Tsinghua University Qian Xuesen Class ESRT | Duration: 2024.12 - Ongoing

Research Content: This project centers on AI digital twin technology, aiming to alleviate the lack of family companionship for elderly people in the context of China's aging population, while bridging the digital divide for seniors and enhancing AI practical capabilities of primary and secondary school students. The project is implemented by Shenzhen X-Institute in collaboration with ByteDance and other institutions, divided into preparation, execution, and promotion phases. It plans to create personalized digital twins for elderly people through students, enhance intergenerational communication, and explore application scenarios of AI large models in social welfare fields.

Supervisor: Tang Min (Former Counselor of the State Council)

Artificial Intelligence Social Innovation Digital Twin Aging Population
