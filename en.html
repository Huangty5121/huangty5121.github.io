Personal Profile

I am Tin-Yeh Huang, currently a student in the Product Engineering with Innovation and Entrepreneurship (Honours) Bachelor's program at the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University. I am also a Zero One Scholar at the Tsinghua University Tsien Excellence in Engineering Program - Shenzhen X-Institute. My research interests span multiple cutting-edge fields including artificial intelligence, mathematics, neuroscience, and computational biology, and I am dedicated to exploring innovative possibilities through interdisciplinary integration.

In academic research, I focus on applying artificial intelligence technologies to the biomedical and social innovation sectors. I have participated in several national-level research projects, including the extraction and analysis of adaptive elements for global heatwave disasters based on multimodal BERT models, and research on the application of AI digital twin technology in addressing elderly companionship issues.

I firmly believe that technology should serve human well-being. Through interdisciplinary research methods, I aim to explore the potential of artificial intelligence in solving practical social problems, hoping to contribute to technological innovation and social progress.

🔬 Research Projects (Public)

Extraction and Analysis of Global Heatwave Disaster Adaptive Elements Based on Multimodal BERT Models

Project Type: 2024 Chinese Academy of Sciences University Student Innovation Practice Training Program (National Project) | Duration: 2024.11 - Ongoing

Research Content: This research integrates text, image, and structured data based on multimodal BERT models, enabling precise identification of key factors affecting heatwave adaptation. The research results will provide solid theoretical and data support for formulating scientific global heatwave response strategies and enhance society's overall disaster adaptation capacity.

Supervisor: Researcher Yong Ge (Institute of Geographic Sciences and Natural Resources Research, CAS, Distinguished Young Scholar)

Large Language Models Spatial Analysis BERT Disaster Management

Research on Elderly Companionship Issues Based on AI Digital Twin Technology

Project Type: Tsinghua University Tsien Program ESRT | Duration: 2024.12 - Ongoing

Research Content: This project centers on AI digital twin technology, aiming to alleviate the lack of emotional companionship for elderly people in the context of China's aging population, while bridging the digital divide for seniors and enhancing AI practical capabilities for primary and secondary school students. The project is implemented jointly by Shenzhen X-Institute and ByteDance, among other institutions, divided into preparation, execution, and promotion phases. It plans to enhance intergenerational communication through students creating personalized digital twins for elderly people, exploring application scenarios of AI large models in social welfare fields.

Supervisor: Min Tang (Former Counselor of the State Council)
